How to win a Nobel Prize

First, get nominated. The more times the better.

Albert Einstein was nominated a total of 62 times.

He won in 1921.

Chemist Linus Pauling garnered 70 nominations throughout his career

He won in 1954....

...and again in 1962.

But no one tops this guy.

Gaston Ramon.

He was nominated for the Nobel Prize in Medicine 155 times, by 136 different people.

He never won.

In 1934, George Minot, George Whipple, and William Murphy shared the Prize even though Ramon got more nominations than the three of them put together.

Ramon was again snubbed in 1935, 1937, and 1938.

How does that happen?

Ramon received 25 nominations for the 1934 prize. They came from here

Minot, Whipple, and Murphy's nominations came from here

Between 1901 and 1966, roughly 1 in every 20 nominations cast by an American produced a prizewinner. When the nominator was French, the success rate was just one in 50.

A nomination from the US is basically worth two and a half from France.

Perhaps Gaston might have fared better had he garnered British support?

Based on the data, a nomination from a Brit is worth 2 1/2 from an American.

A nomination from Sweden was worth 3 from the US.

If you've really got to have that Nobel, befriend a Swede from Uppsala.

One in every four nominations from an Uppsalan results in a Nobel laureate.

Sometimes it’s not who you know but where they're at.
